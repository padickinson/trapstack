Trap Pile

Also known as Mousetrap Jenga.

Trap Pile is the second major TrapStack variant. It’s turn-based, fast, and a little mean. Players take turns stacking loaded mousetraps on a plate until someone sets off the pile.

How we played it

  • Players take turns placing one loaded trap on the plate
  • If you knock a trap off the plate, you must replace it
  • If a few traps go off but the pile doesn’t collapse, you replace those too
  • The game ends when someone sets off the pile

The tension comes from the plate: there’s no floor to sprawl across, so every placement is a judgment call. It feels like Jenga, but with consequences.

Practical note:
Use a sturdy plate or tray with a flat base. You want a little bite, not a full detonation.
